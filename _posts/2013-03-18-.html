Holy shit. Did I accomplish anything today?

Finally, you've realized you're no longer a dev, you're a lead. Pour yourself a drink, it's about to get interesting.

At Palantir, we have a lead training course, but nothing can prepare you for that moment when you finally realize you aren't a dev any more. It sounds scary and awful and it takes awhile to get used to, but its actually a liberating moment.

If you're like me or any of the other leads I've spoken to, the story goes a bit like this.

I don't know why my boss has no time to write code. I have a few reports, I'm responsible for a couple projects, but its not that bad. I still code 60-80% of the time. I'll just work a bit longer and all will be well.

And then you start getting involved in hiring. Someone on your team is has a freak out moment. You gain a few more people and a few more projects. You visit your customer more regularly. You actually follow through on your weekly 1:1s. Next thing you know...

Fuck. I promised that code for the next release and I'm not even close. I've even barely opened my editor.

Take a breath. Don't freak out. If your lead is worth anything at all, he's seen this moment coming and won't be surprised, nor disappointed.

You didn't get into this position by being irresponsible, tell someone that you're screwed, figure something out and lets move on.

Hmm. But now I'm worthless. I just talk to people all day, do email, and read code.

Yup, except your not worthless. You now have a huge new challenge in front of you, one more challenging than coding: hacking people. You see, you were put into this position because you have something you can pass on to others.
